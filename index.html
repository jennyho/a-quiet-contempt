Examining the Stigma of Mental Illness

scroll down

In the context of mental health, stigma is not a physical mark. Instead, it persists as beliefs the public holds against individuals affected by mental illness. Despite the public discourse on mental health and the many initiatives aimed to reduce stigma, it still is a major barrier for those looking for support and treatment.

In the United States...

43.6 million adults had some form of mental illness in the past year.

9.8 million had a serious mental illness that led to severe functional impairment.

55% of people with any mental illness did not seek treatment.

Of those who do, 20% quit prematurely.

Of those who didn’t seek treatment, 97% cite reasons related to social and personal attitudes.

Of those dropped out of treatment, 89% also cited social stigma as a reason.
